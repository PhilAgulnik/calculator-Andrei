Which best describes your employment status?

If you are both employed and self-employed, select 'Self-employed' if more than half your income is from self-employment. Otherwise select 'Employed'.
If you are not employed but have worked in the last 2 years we will ask for more information to see if you could get help based on your national insurance contributions.

If you are the Director of a company and its sole employee (ie, it is a service company for you only) then you are treated as self-employed in the benefits system and you should choose the 'Self-employed' option. If you have employees stay on the 'Director of your own company' option, as you are treated as an employee of the company. The rules are complicated, so please read our help page on Company directors and how they are treated in the benefits system.

Do you receive a disability or sickness benefit?

Common disability benefits are Attendance Allowance, Pension Age Disability Payment, Disability Living Allowance (DLA), Personal Independence Payment (PIP), Adult Disability Payment, Employment and Support Allowance (ESA), Universal Credit limited capability for work element (LCW/RA) and Statutory Sick Pay (SSP).

People who have claimed disability benefits in the last 6 months and work 16 or more hours a week may qualify for extra help through Working Tax Credit disability element. We will ask whether you qualify later in the calculator.

Are you ill or disabled but not claiming one of the disability benefits listed below?

Answer 'yes' if you have have a health condition that affects your daily living or mobility and would like to find out more about Personal Independence Payment, Adult Disability Payment or Attendance Allowance.

Do you care for someone who is sick or disabled?

If you spend at least 35 hours a week caring and earn less than a limit you may qualify for Carer's Allowance or a Carer Support Payment in Scotland. The earnings limit is currently £196 a week and is calculated after any childcare or replacement care costs while working If you are claiming Universal Credit you may get a carer element even if you have earnings above this limit. We will ask more questions later in the calculator to check whether you qualify.
