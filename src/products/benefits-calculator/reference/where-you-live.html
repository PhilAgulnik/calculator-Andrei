Which best describes your current housing status?

If you are part of a couple and one of you lives in residential care on a permanent basis you will usually be treated as separate single claimants rather than claiming benefits as a couple. This may mean you need to carry out two separate benefit calculations, one for each member of the couple, splitting any joint capital. If you are a couple and you both live in residential care please get advice from an experienced adviser. If your landlord provides you with support services, supervision or care then your home may be classified as supported accommodation. Various exemptions from welfare reforms, such as the benefit cap, apply to people living in supported accommodation. If you are in doubt about your housing status please read our information on supported exempt accommodation and/or check with your landlord.
