If someone in your household is ill, or you have been told to self-isolate for another reason, you may be entitled to some help.

Employed? If your employer does not offer paid sick leave you may be able to claim Statutory Sick Pay (SSP). In the next few pages of the calculator please:

  • Choose 'Current claim' for the question ‘Do you receive a disability or sickness benefit ?’ followed by ‘Statutory Sick Pay’ in the list provided.
  • Enter your current work hours as 0.

Self-employed or not eligible for SSP? Any entitlements will be based on both the circumstances of your household as well as your previous work history and national insurance contributions. As you go through the calculator we will check these details and work out which benefits you may be entitled to.

Already claiming benefits? Please read the coronavirus help page (click the i above) as guidance on what you need to report varies depending of what benefits you are already claiming.

If your income has gone down because you (or someone else in your household) are working fewer hours, have been asked to take unpaid leave, or any self-employed earnings have reduced, you may be entitled to claim some benefits.

Your entitlements will be based on the circumstances of your entire household, so as you go through the calculator we will ask about your circumstances to be able to work out which benefits you may be entitled to.

Are you in hospital/residential care, a prisoner, on strike, living abroad or a full-time student?

Please note: If you’re a full time student, in hospital, a prisoner, on strike or living abroad we cannot guarantee the accuracy of our estimates. As the calculator does not calculate entitlements accurately for these groups we recommend seeing an adviser to understand your personal circumstances.
