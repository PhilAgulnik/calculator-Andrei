Universal Credit and self-employment

To calculate UC correctly for self-employed people need to calculate the Minimum Income Floor (MIF). For this we need to add extra variables:
- ‘UCWorkHoursConditionality’
- ‘UCEarningsThreshold’
- ‘UCMinimumIncomeFloor’
Where: 
‘UCEarningsThreshold’ if single = UCWorkHoursConditionality * minimum wage for their age (deduction for tax and NI allowed)
‘UCEarningsThreshold’  if couple = client (UCWorkHoursConditionality * minimum wage for their age) + partner (UCWorkHoursConditionality * minimum wage for their age)

Single people
Aim – 
When a single person enters self-employed income (on the net earnings page) that is below their appropriate minimum income floor we will show them a message and use the appropriate MIF figure as their income in the UC calculation instead of the self-employed income they have entered.
What is the minimum income floor -
A person’s minimum income floor is their individual threshold multiplied by the national minimum wage.
What is the individual threshold -
•	0 hours per week if person would otherwise be subject to no work-related requirements (child under 1, carer, PC age, adopted within 12 months, pregnant (usual circs), some young people)
•	16 hours if person would otherwise be subject to work-preparation or work-focused interview requirement only (people assessed as LCW, child aged 1-5 currently, foster parents with child aged up to 16 or 18 in some circs)
•	35 hours if person would otherwise be subject to all work related requirements – though can be reduced in some circs around caring responsibilities or health

Working out the minimum income floor - 
The individual threshold (0, 16 or 35) is then multiplied by the NMW at that time to give us the person’s MIF.
Deciding which figure to use as income –
The MIF needs to be compared to the amount the person entered as self-employed income.
If the MIF is greater than the self-employed income entered by the user then the MIF should be used as income in the UC calculation instead of the self-employed income entered by the user.
If the MIF is less than the self-employed income entered by the user then the self-employed income entered by the user should be used in the UC calculation.

Couples 
If one or both members of a couple are self-employed then each member’s MIF will be worked out separately and then added together to provide a combined MIF.
Each member’s income from self-employment (or earnings from employment if one is employed and one self-employed) will be added together to provide a combined income.
If the combined MIF is greater than the combined income then the combined MIF is used as income in the UC calculation instead of the self-employed income and/or employed earnings entered by the user.
If the combined MIF is less than the combined income, then the self-employed income and/or employed earnings entered by the user are used in the UC calculation.
